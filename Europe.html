My European destinations

Edinbrough

In 2018, I had the pleasure of spending a weekend in Edinburgh, Scotland. The city was bustling with energy and excitement, and I was immediately struck by the friendly locals and stunning architecture.

I arrived on Friday evening and checked into my hotel before heading out to explore the city. One of my first stops was the Royal Mile, a historic street lined with charming shops, restaurants, and pubs. I also visited the famous Edinburgh Castle, which provided an incredible panoramic view of the city.

On Saturday, I joined a walking tour of the city and learned about the rich history and culture of Edinburgh. I also visited the National Museum of Scotland, which was filled with fascinating exhibits on the country's history, science, and culture.

In the evening, I explored the city's nightlife and visited some of the local pubs and bars. The atmosphere was lively, and the live music and friendly locals made for a memorable experience.

On Sunday, I took a hike up Arthur's Seat and enjoyed the breathtaking views of the city and surrounding countryside. I also visited the Scottish National Gallery, which was filled with incredible works of art from both local and international artists.

Overall, my weekend in Edinburgh was a wonderful experience, and I can't wait to return someday and explore more of this incredible city. From the history and culture to the food and nightlife, there's something for everyone in Edinburgh.

Sevilla

My 5-day vacation in Seville was an unforgettable experience filled with amazing sights, sounds, and flavors. From the moment I arrived, I was struck by the city's beauty and energy.

On the first day, I visited the Alcazar of Seville, a magnificent palace that is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The palace's intricate architecture and stunning gardens were truly awe-inspiring. In the evening, I walked along the Guadalquivir River and watched the sunset over the city.

The second day was spent exploring the historic Santa Cruz neighborhood, with its winding streets and picturesque plazas. I visited the Seville Cathedral, the largest Gothic cathedral in the world, and climbed the Giralda Tower for panoramic views of the city.

Berlin

My 5-day vacation in Berlin was not great, not terrible. While I was excited to explore the city and experience its culture, the trip didn't quite live up to my expectations.

On the first day, I visited the iconic Brandenburg Gate and took a walking tour of the city. While the history and architecture were fascinating, I found the city to be crowded and somewhat overwhelming.

The second day was spent exploring museums and galleries, which were impressive but left me feeling somewhat uninspired. The highlight of the day was a visit to the Berlin Wall, which was a powerful reminder of the city's tumultuous past.

On the third day, I took a bike tour of the city and visited some of its lesser-known neighborhoods. While I appreciated the opportunity to see a different side of Berlin, I found the tour to be somewhat lackluster.

The fourth day was spent wandering around the city, trying to find something that really resonated with me. While I enjoyed some of the food and drink, I found myself feeling disappointed by the overall experience.

On the final day, I visited the East Side Gallery, an outdoor gallery featuring murals painted on a remaining section of the Berlin Wall. While I appreciated the art and the history, I left the city feeling somewhat underwhelmed by the overall experience.

In conclusion, while my 5-day vacation in Berlin was not great, not terrible, it didn't quite meet my expectations. While I appreciate the city's rich history and culture, I found the experience to be somewhat uninspiring and underwhelming.
